Mustafa Kemal ATATÜRK

Founder and the first president of the Turkish Republic

Here is a short look of a great leader's life:

  • 1881 - Mustafa Kemal Atatürk was born in Salonica (Thessaloniki), then part of the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1893 - He enrolled in the military school in Salonica, where he received the name "Kemal" from his mathematics teacher, meaning "perfection" or "maturity."
  • 1899 - He joined the Ottoman Military Academy in Istanbul, where he continued his education in military strategy and leadership.
  • 1905 - He graduated as a staff captain from the Ottoman Military Academy and was assigned to Damascus, where he was exposed to revolutionary ideas.
  • 1908 - He played a role in the Young Turk Revolution, which aimed to restore constitutional rule in the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1911 - He participated in the Italo-Turkish War in Libya, where he gained experience in modern warfare and guerrilla tactics.
  • 1915 - He became a national hero during the Gallipoli Campaign by successfully leading Ottoman forces against the Allied invasion.
  • 1916 - He was promoted to the rank of general and played a key role in repelling Russian advances on the Eastern Front.
  • 1919 - He landed in Samsun, marking the beginning of the Turkish War of Independence against occupying Allied forces.
  • 1920 - He led the establishment of the Grand National Assembly in Ankara, creating a new government separate from the Ottoman Sultanate.
  • 1921 - He secured victory against Greek forces at the Battle of Sakarya, solidifying the Turkish resistance movement.
  • 1922 - He led the final push against Greek forces in the Battle of Dumlupınar, ensuring Turkey's independence.
  • 1923 - He officially founded the Republic of Turkey and became its first president, abolishing the Ottoman Sultanate.
  • 1924 - He abolished the Caliphate, further separating religion from the newly formed secular state.
  • 1928 - He introduced the Latin alphabet for the Turkish language, replacing the Arabic script to modernize education and communication.
  • 1930 - He encouraged multi-party democracy by allowing the formation of the Free Republican Party, though it was short-lived.
  • 1934 - He initiated major reforms in women's rights, granting Turkish women the right to vote and run for office.
  • 1935 - He adopted the surname "Atatürk," meaning "Father of the Turks," under the Surname Law.
  • 1937 - He implemented economic and social reforms to modernize Turkey further, focusing on industrialization and education.
  • 1938 - He passed away in Dolmabahçe Palace, Istanbul, leaving behind a transformed, independent, and secular Turkey.
